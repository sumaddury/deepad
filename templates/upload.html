DeepAD

18F-Florbetapir PET Amyloid-Beta Quantification

This application uses co-registered 18F-AV-45 (florbetapir) PET scans and an EfficientNet based deep learning model to quantify amyloid deposition. Amyloid is a misfolded protein that can be seen in PET scans via the binding radiotracer 18F-Florbetapir. Amyloid plaques are generally found in Alzheimer's patients, and thus the quantity of amyloid deposition is immensely useful for the early diagnosis of Alzheimer's disease. Today however, many hospitals require specialized neuroradiologists and/or expensive equipment to quantify amyloid, and so we created a specialized convolutional neural network imaging algorithm to accurately estimate amyloid--measured in Standardized-Uptake-Value-Ratio (SUVR)--with less than 4% margin of error. This application is intended for medical professionals and requires a specialized type of scan (18F-Florbetapir PET).

Basic Workflow:
1. Obtain full axial 18F-Florbetapir PET scan of designated patient.
2. Co-register PET scan with MRI scan.
3. Extract slices 36, 48, and 60 on a 96 slice scan (or the slice 3/8, 1/5, and 5/8 of the way)
4. Standardize voxel size and scale image resolution to 224 * 224 pixels.
5. Overlay the three images on top of each other to produce one image 6. Upload to the "Choose File" option and hit "Predict".
